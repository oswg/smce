We've entered 1977, and it will be a very brief visit as there are [only six or so documented contacts in the archive](https://www.llresearch.org/channeling/1977/1). I would love to know the reason, but I'm not sure there's anybody still around who could explain it -- remember that Jim McCarty doesn't join LLR until 1980ish.

Here we have Hatonn addressing the subject of the body, a subject that has philosophically interested me for some time. When Ra [says](https://www.lawofone.info/s/81#14) that the body is the creature of the mind and an instrument for the manifestation of the fruits of the other complexes, what is the import of this statement? It seems to be in part a crucible of balance, in which one must be judicious about how the fruits of beingness are seated in consciousness such that they blossom into the physical experience. 

Yet if these fruits are the object, what is the subject? This is to me a very important detail of Hatonn's remarks: that because we can think of the body as a kindly vehicle, we can conceive of an identity that transcends it. It strikes me that it is in this transcendence that one can weigh the stakes of manifestation properly, and learn about the infinite self through this experience of limitation. If I'm not terribly far off in my thinking, only by balancing this vehicle of manifestation can this ingress result in a grounded, complete learning returned to the Creator.

What a pickle it is to be both vehicle and driver, both instrument and user, both Creator and creature!

[As I've talked about before](https://youtu.be/JNvsbuAxCiE?si=k0PcKe00ZlDgbpr9), I tend to think of the physical experience as a kind of inflection point for the ingress of spirit/intelligent infinity into the drama of intelligent energy as it is released or conserved at a given moment. If we think of manifestation as this kind of limiting of the self such that this concentrated separation can penetrate the total mystery of the Creator, then we can think of the physical as the deepest point of ingress, the point at which the penetration must reflect back to the Creator.

> You see, my brothers, you are not on the same order of the apple tree. You are not out in the weather. You do not grow, wither and die. You are a consciousness that is not born and does not die. You are joy. To touch this place within yourself is all-important at this time, for if you can but make contact with your higher self, you can be of great comfort and of great service to many in the days to come. For in the days to come, as we have said many times, there will be many who are afraid, and many who have put off their voyage of self-discovery until the very last minute and who, when they finally realize that they must now decide who they are, will be absolutely terrified.
